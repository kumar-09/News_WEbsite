Srinagar: Congress leader Rahul Gandhi on Tuesday broke his silence on senior party leader Digvijaya Singh's "surgical strikes" remark that has set off a political controversy.

Responding to a question on Singh's remarks at a press conference, Gandhi said the views of the party are above the senior Congress leader.

Digvijaya Singh on Monday kicked up a controversy by seeking proof of the Uri surgical strikes on terror camps in Pakistan, saying that the Central government always talks about the strikes but never gives any evidence.

"We don't appreciate Digvijaya Singh's personal views. The views of the party are above Digvijaya Singh. The views of the party are generated from a conversation within the party. I would like to assure you that Digvijaya Singh's views are outlying views. They are not the views that are held by the main centre of the party," he said at the media briefing on the sidelines of Bharat Jodo Yatra in Jammu and Kashmir.
