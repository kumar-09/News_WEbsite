Gujarat BJP MLA terms Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose 'aatankwadi' on FB, blames 'incorrect translation' for error

After some people spotted the error, they informed the Anand MLA the about the choice of words. The legislator immediately deleted the post.

Anand: Several political leaders on Monday took to their social media platforms to pay tributes to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose on his birth anniversary. A BJP MLA from Gujarat was also among many who remembered Bose and put up a post on his Facebook page as a tribute to him on his birth anniversary.
Yogesh R Patel (Bapji), who represents Anand Assembly constituency in Gujarat, in his post stated Netaji was a member of an “aatankwadi" (terrorist) wing. The MLA, however, later apologised and blamed the incorrect translation for the mistake.

"Bose was a member of aatankwadi pankh" (wing). He participated in the civil disobedience movement as a Congress leader, and was known for advocating socialist policies,” Patel shared a post in Gujarati on his Facebook page.

After some people spotted the error, they informed the Anand MLA the about the choice of words. The legislator immediately deleted the post.
Even as Patel took down his post, the Gujarat AAP slammed the BJP MLA for the mistake and sought his apology.
